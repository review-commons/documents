REVIEW COMMONS

Review Commons is a platform for high-quality journal-independent peer-review in the life sciences.

To learn more about the scope of Review Commons and its review process, consult our Guidelines for Authors and Guidelines for Reviewers.

The following journals are affiliated with Review Commons: eLife, Journal of Cell Biology, EMBO Journal, EMBO reports, Molecular Systems Biology, EMBO Molecular Medicine, Life Science Alliance, Development, Journal of Cell Science, Disease Models & Mechanisms, Biology Open, PLOS Biology, PLOS Genetics, PLOS Computational Biology, PLOS Pathogens, PLOS One, Molecular Biology of the Cell.

This site allows you to submit manuscripts to Review Commons and monitor the status of your submission. If at any time you need assistance, please contact the journal office atÂ contact@reviewcommons.org

There is no need to follow a particular journal format to submit to Review Commons.

An initial editorial assessment will be carried out by the Managing Editor in consultation with the Advisory Board to select manuscripts that will be peer reviewed.

As of August 1, 2021, Review Commons will require all authors to post their manuscript as a preprint, prior to transfer to an affiliate journal. In return, all the affiliate journals provide authors with scooping protection from the date of posting of the preprint. Note that posting of the reviews ('refereed preprint') remains optional at this time. See our guidelines for further details on these policies.

Note: at a later stage, once the peer review process is complete, authors are provided with the opportunity and strongly encouraged to post the referee comments and a reply to the comments alongside the preprint.

If you already have a preprint posted, it is easier for you to transfer the preprint to Review Commons through the bioRxiv or medRixv submission systems (https://submit.biorxiv.org/ and https://submit.medrxiv.org/). The files will then be automatically transferred, and you do not need to reenter author information.

If you prefer to submit your manuscript directly, please proceed through the next steps. You will also be given the opportunity to post your manuscript as a preprint during submission.
